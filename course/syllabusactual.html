    Section Course Information

    Subsection Course Description

    A thorough introduction to the application of statistics to the sciences. Topics include a brief introduction to the appropriate descriptive statistics, relevant probability distributions, and a heavy focus on regression and hypothesis testing, including t-tests, chi-square tests for categorical data, and analysis of variance. Emphasis is placed on the analysis of data in order to apply an appropriate inferential test. Appropriate statistical software is employed.
    🔗
    🔗

    Section Course Learning Outcomes

    By the end of the course, you will be able to:
    🔗
    • Turn research problems into statistical questions.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Identify the structure of a study and explain how this influences potential conclusions.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Construct graphics from data and describe what they reveal about the data.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Compute summary statistics and explain what they communicate about the data in plain language.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Select and apply an appropriate statistical method for a given dataset.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Communicate the results of a statistical analysis.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Use statistical software for data summarization and analysis.

    Section Career Readiness

    This course prepares you for your career by addressing the following National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE) competencies:
    🔗
    Problem-Solving & Critical Thinking: Your will learn to translate research questions into statistical queries, analyze study designs, and select appropriate inferential tests, fostering analytical reasoning and decision-making essential for all careers.
    🔗
    Quantitative & Technical Skills: The course emphasizes computing summary statistics, constructing informative graphics, and using statistical software (RStudio), preparing you to handle and interpret complex data accurately.
    🔗
    Data Interpretation & Communication: You will practice explaining statistical results in plain language and constructing clear visualizations, enhancing written and verbal communication skills necessary for reporting and collaboration.
    🔗
    Teamwork & Professionalism: By working with data in realistic contexts, you will develop collaborative and ethical approaches to research, aligning with NACE competencies of teamwork, professionalism, and ethical responsibility.
    🔗
    Career Readiness & Lifelong Learning: Mastery of statistical tools, data interpretation, and software proficiency equips you to pursue further research, graduate studies, or technical roles, fostering adaptability and continuous learning in your career.
    🔗
    For more information on NACE competencies visit: https://www.naceweb.org/career-readiness/competencies/career-readiness-defined/
    🔗

    Section Assessments and Grading Criteria

    Your grade in the course will be determined by your proficiency on the following assessments:
    🔗
    1. Lecture Activities (20%)
      🔗
      These activities give you an opportunity to practice what you’ve learned during lecture time. They will take a variety of forms including group work and mini presentations. These activities cannot be made up if you are absent since they are completed during lecture. The lowest two scores will be dropped.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Exams (16% each, 80% total)
      🔗
      Exams are an opportunity to show what you have learned and assess your understanding of the content. You are allowed one two-sided sheet of hand-written notes, and the instructor will provide a formula sheet if deemed necessary. If you miss an exam, you must schedule and take a make-up exam within 5 business days of the original exam date. It is your responsibility to schedule the make-up. A penalty of 20% of the overall grade will be applied for each business day that the exam is late.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Readings and Homework
      🔗
      You are highly encouraged to complete the reading and homework assigned with each lecture for practice. You can receive feedback and ask questions about these during office hours or by appointment.

    Subsection Academic Integrity

    Unless otherwise noted, students are allowed and encouraged to collaborate on assignments. However, any work that you turn in for grading needs to be your own. You are welcome to use internet resources to supplement content we cover in this course, with the exception of solutions to homework problems. Copying solutions from the internet is a violation of the honor principle. Using AI technologies in inappropriate manners is also a violation. This includes using AI to think for you and completing assignments with answers and information completely derived from AI technologies. You are welcome to learn from AI technologies, but all work submitted should be your original work created and worded with no help from AI. Exams will explicitly mention what resources may be consulted. All written work that references material outside of the textbook or lecture should be accompanied by an appropriate citation.

    Subsection Illness and Absences

    When your health allows, you are expected to attend lectures. At the same time, we all have a responsibility to help prevent the spread of COVID-19 and other infectious diseases. Following public health guidance is part of living in an honorable community. Self-isolation is recommended for anyone experiencing flu-like symptoms, whether due to possible coronavirus or to other illnesses. Please stay at home if you feel sick and contact the Health Center - Foster Hall or your healthcare provider to discuss. If you test positive for COVID-19, you should self-isolate and not attend lectures for at least five full days. If your symptoms have resolved on Day 6 and you have had no fever for 24 hours without using fever-reducing medication, you may leave isolation and wear a mask. You may stop wearing a mask before Day 11 if you have 2 negative tests taken 48 hours apart. You can find more information on FSU COVID policies at https://www.framingham.edu/about-fsu/news-and-events/covid-19-safety-protocols.html.
